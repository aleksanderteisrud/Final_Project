    Our solution for this problem is an app, which has a lot of benefits! The whole idea is based on a point-system where you get points for doing something good for the environment, for example public transport. When you take the bus, you pull out your phone to scan a QR-code (for example on the ticket/receipt or similar). When you collect a certain amount of points, donations will be given to the poor. The money will come from sponsors. The sponsors will have a chance to improve their public relations, and they will get ads on the app.

    The app will also contain a lot of achievements available for unlocking by reaching a certain amount of points, or by doing specific actions. This will make the user-experience better.

    Benefits for everyone!

    The app has a lot of great outcomes. It will first encourage the users do a lot of environmentally friendly actions. The outcome of these actions will mean money to the organisations, this will lead to the poor getting more help. On top of everything this will be entertaining due to the achievements you will receive in the app.

    How does the application work?

    The application can be downloaded on your device very soon! It will support most of the softwares, and devices exisiting today.

    Helping Hand is connected to the camera on your device, and scans your receipts and tickets to give you points in the scoring system. You can also download a picture of your receipts and tickets from the camera roll if you’re not connected to the internet. When you’ve reached a certain amount of points, you’ll get a notification and can choose who you want to donate the money to. You can connect to Facebook and compete with your friends on who has been the most environmentally friendly. This can be seen on the scoreboard site in the app. This will make it entertaining to use the app, and with weekly challenges there’s always a reason to keep doing something for the environment.
